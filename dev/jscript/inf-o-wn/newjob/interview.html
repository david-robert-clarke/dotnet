  12. q. Can you explain the differences between SOAK and Performance testing?

  16. q. Can you explain risk based testing?
    17. a. Risk based testing takes the risk to the business for a business function and the complexity of the code and creates a map of which areas are of the highest risk to the project that can then be used to prioritize test execution and focus

  22. q. Can you explain the difference between retesting and regression testing?
    23. a. Retesting – Is rerunning the same test to make sure that a fault/defect has been resolved. b. Regression testing – is performed after a software change or an environment change to make sure that the application still functions as expected.
  23. q. Can you explain the average percentage of code test coverage in System testing vs User acceptance testing?

Agile

  1. q. You are working in a project environment that has constant changing requirements, which methodology and why would suit this type of project?
    2. a. AGILE, this enable a test driven project enabling a stable base to be created and ROI for the business by enabling small sprints and empowering the stakeholders using a product backlog
  2. q. Can you explain what the main individual roles are in Scrum Agile :?
    3. a. the "ScrumMaster", who maintains the processes (typically in lieu of a project manager) b. the "Product Owner", who represents the stakeholders and the business c. the "Team", a cross-functional group of about 7 people who do the actual analysis, design, implementation, testing, etc.
  3. q. Can you explain the difference in AGILE between a “Pig” role, and a “Chicken” Role?
    4. a. Pig Roles – ScrumMaster, Team, Product Owner b. Chicken Roles – Stakeholders, Environment Managers
  4. q. Can you explain what an AGILE sprint is?
    5. a. In product development, a Scrum sprint is a set period of time during which specific work has to be completed and made ready for review
  5. q. Can you explain the term Sprint Planning and when it takes place?
    6. a. At the beginning of the sprint cycle (every 7–30 days), a “Sprint Planning Meeting” is held. b. Prepare the Sprint Backlog that details the time it will take to do that work, with the entire team c. Identify and communicate how much of the work is likely to be done during the current sprint
  6. q. What is Planning Poker used for in an AGILE environment?
    7. a. Individual user stories are presented for estimation by the Scrum Master. After a period of discussion, each participant chooses from his own deck the numbered card that represents his estimate of how much work is involved in the story under discussion. All estimates are kept private until each participant has chosen a card. At that time, all estimates are revealed and discussion can begin again

Bums
By Ed Starkie BSc

As in the case of your legs, the main culprit for causing the deposits around the bottom area is saturated fat in the diet. It is advisable to cut it out of your diet if possible, as it is bad for you in a variety of different ways. However, it is not necessary to go on a crash diet in order to go some way towards shifting these deposits. It is merely necessary to cut out the rubbish that you eat and replace it with quality nutrients which will aid your body's metabolism.

Again, as with shifting cellulite around your thighs, an increase in activity is recommended. However, for the purpose of firming up your buttocks (Gluteals), there are a few aerobic exercises which will not only burn fat but work the Gluteal muscles as well. The first of these is rowing which gives a good all round workout, helping you to burn a large majority of fat if you exercise at around 40% of your theoretical maximum heart rate (use 220- your age for this calculation). However, it also uses your buttocks in the rowing action and therefore has a significant firming effect. The same can be said for swimming. As well as being an extremely good exercise for burning fat (due to the fact that you are usually working at a relatively low intensity) the leg action involves the buttocks in every stroke, especially the breaststroke.

Finally you will need to carry out some buttock-specific exercises as follows:

Mule Kicks

When it comes to buttock-specific exercises, one of the best is the mule kick (or donkey-kick). Position yourself on all fours on the floor using a mat to protect your knees. Supporting yourself on one knee, bring the other up towards your chest and through towards your chin. Then push it backwards and upwards, straightening your leg in the process until it is pointing up into the air. At this point, squeeze your buttock muscles and hold for 2 seconds before releasing and repeating the exercise. This should be done 10 times on both legs. This exercise, if done correctly, should give you a real burn.

Seated Squats

As mentioned in the 'Legs' section of this week's Focus. This exercise is also known as the Leg Press and is extremely good for all muscle groups in the legs, but particularly the thighs and gluteals. Also it can be performed with less than bodyweight as resistance which is safer than performing normal squats and creates less stress around the knee joint.

Step-Ups

As well as being generally good for the thighs, this exercise can also be adapted to benefit the gluteals. When performing the step-up, the buttocks can be exercised by tensing your trailing leg buttock just as you push off the floor. Again, this exercise should be repeated around 10 times per leg and intensity can be increased with extra reps or by carrying weights.

Leg Abductions

Abductions (so called because they exercises the Abductor muscles which run down the outside of your thighs) involve lying on your side with your whole body in-line, then raising your top leg up in the air as high as possible. The leg should then be returned to a horizontal position without letting it touch the lower leg (this ensures that the upper leg abductor is constantly exercised). This should be repeated on both legs 10 times. This exercise not only exercises the abductors but also the gluteals when your leg is at the top of its lift.

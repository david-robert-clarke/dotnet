# Frederick Clarke's Biography

## Introduction

For anybody who doesn't know me... my name is David Clarke, Frederick was my dad. In this Eulogy I'm going to cover Frederick's life:

1. In the early days.
2. With his family.
3. What he meant to me.

## Main

Frederick was born Frederick Charles Clarke on April 12th 1943 in 103 Couchman Road, Alum Rock, Birmingham. The eldest of his 3 siblings,

### Holidays

When Fred was 18 he went on a trip to Ibiza, travelling from Brum on the train down to Dover. Catching the ferry across the channel to Calais. From Calais he took a train down to Barcelona. In Barcelona he lodged in the basement of a Spanish bloke’s house on Raglan Road (the tourist centre of Barcelona). From there they waited for the next available ferry to Ibiza. They stayed in Ibiza town.

Fred and Holmesey travelled back from Blackpool to Brum, but got stuck in Wolverhampton. With it being brisk (cold), they both took shelter in an electrically heated waiting room. However, the heater didn’t heat all the time. When it did shut off, they both froze.

### Sporting Achievements

Best in his secondary school at:

- football
- swimming (50 metres)
- distance running (half mile)
- long jump.

Fred played in defence for Spark Brook United straight after finishing school. He met good mates there.

### School

Anthony Road Infants & Junior School Alston Road Secondary School (Left at age 15 years)

### Work

Just before his 15th birthday Fred finished school. He turned 15 on the Saturday and started his first job on the Monday at Metro Camel. Fred worked as a gopher. He'd shadow engineers who would ask him "go fetch me this, go fetch me that". Fred was always on the look out for new jobs to earn a little extra. He worked for, amongst many other, the following engineering companies during his career:

1. Metro Cammel
2. Birmingham Small Arms - sheet metal worker
3. Site Cruise
4. Whale Tankers - welder and electrician(12 years)

### Early Life with Charlie

Grandad Charlie had a truck that supposedly he owned. He was often travelling from A to B to deliver goods. Dad used to go along for the ride on the weekends to Bristol. He described an incident where Charlie crashed the truck during a drive to Nottingham while transporting coal.

### Fred Saved a Kid from Drowning

Fred aged 11, travelled to Kingsbury waterpark with his next door neighbour, Charlie Brakewell. Dad was sat at the side of lake with his swimming trunks on. He saw a kid in difficulties in the water Close in, 10 metres away, Dad saw the kid (8 or 9 years old) he rushed in grabbed him  and brought him in swimming back stroke.

### Questions

- "What is your greatest regret?" Something confusing about mom and a baby she'd lost. Unfortunately his mind later stored a confusing array of mixed up stories. Some true, some false and some just in the wrong time line.
- "What were your hopes and dreams as a child?" He had none. He just went with the flow.
- "What would you like to see change in the next 10 years?" Better weather.
- "What was the most rebellious thing you did as a young person?" He said that him and his siblings were well behaved. He knew his place. If he misbehaved he knew he had a clout coming from father Charlie.
- What is the one piece of advice you would like to share with me? Don't eat orange snow.
- What thing in your life made you the happiest or the proudest? Getting the first job and getting to the stage where I was good at it.
- What is your biggest regret? What would you have done differently? Not getting an apprenticeship. He could have walked into more jobs and get more skilled, more money.
- What event had the biggest impact on you? Getting married and having kids. Would you believe that Dad was the one who was asking the question to mom about "When would be a good time to start a family?"
- What plans should we make for the future? To live a happy life.
- What one thing would make me appear a success in your eyes? Dave, when you can take me to the Mercedes show room and buy whichever car I wanted. When bringing up the conversation around cars, Dad would talk about how he’d love to have a Merc like his best mate, Alan Smith.
- What was the trendiest item to have "back in the day"? Tom Waters Suits
- What do you want to be remembered for? Being strong and reliable
- What was the scariest thing you've ever done? He couldn't remember.
- What was your favourite food? It used to be roe and chips. The stew Winnie used to do with dumplings. His recent favourite meal is chicken chow mein.
- Which interesting places have you visited? Blankenberge, when out of work as a teenager. Him and his mate met some girls from a Hebden Bridge. He has visited the - Costa Brava and Costa Del Sol.
- Did you love mom? ...your mom was a great person to talk to. A good friend.
- What interesting stories did you have about Charlie? He was a good amateur boxer. As a kid, when dad beat up one of the Cadman's and ran to the bottom of his garden. - Cadman's dad, a "hard man", ran after him. Charlie came to Dad's aid, walking out of the kitchen and threatening Cadman. Charlie loved more than anything to go to - Church.
- Which people does he respect, admire? He admire footballers, they earn loads of money and surround themselves with attractive women.
- What is your favourite TV show? Strictly Come Dancing
- What is your very first memory? Getting his bike stolen by the rag man.
- What was your neighborhood like? At 6pm each day I had to go to Chilcotts to fetch the bread. I was scared of fetching the bread. The cadmans would be waiting for - me. There were 5 brothers waiting for me to give me a hiding. Johnny had one arm. Once a week I had to fetch the coke from the coke yard. I would put it in a pram - and a bag. I had to go to the pub to get a bottle of mild for old man Millman.
- What was your relationship like with your parents? Charlie was the boss, the person to clout you if you did wrong. Dad got on with Winnie better. Winnie was the - generous one. If Fred was out of pocket, she'd offer him money out the kindness of her heart. At the age of 21, Winnie and Charlie bought Fred a Smith branded watch, - which cost £21, as a present. Dad would protect Winnie, if Hazel was cheeky to Winnie, Dad would give Hazel a clout.
- What was it like growing up with your siblings or without siblings? Did they fight? What was their favorite game to play together? Hazel was cheeky. He got on well - with rest of his siblings.
- What kind of food did you like growing up? What was your favorite meal? Winnie’s stew with dumplings
- Who were your role models and why? Footballers, they had money and women.
- Who were your favorite celebrities? David Jason. What were your favorite movies? Treasure Island, Jack Hawkins. Song? Cathy’s clown.
- What was the world like when you grew up? Rough.
- Did any major newsworthy events happen during your childhood? When sputnik was sent into space on the radio.
- What was the most difficult part of your childhood? There wasn't a difficult part.
- What is your favorite memory from your childhood? When England won the world cup.
- What was your first meal of the day as a kid? First meal was chips and beans at 12:30pm.
- Who, in history, would you be proud to say you're a descendant of? My father Charlie. He was an honest man. I wish he'd taught me how to box.
